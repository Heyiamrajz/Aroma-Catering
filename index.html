Problem & Solution

The host shouldn’t be stuck in the kitchen

Hosting a cultural event in Finland often means cooking in a cramped apartment kitchen for 40–60 people. It’s stressful, time consuming, and most families have no licensed, affordable alternative.

The problem

Big celebrations, tiny kitchens

Nepali and Indian families in Finland organise regular cultural gatherings, but their homes are not designed for large-volume cooking. People end up cooking for days and still worry about quality and food safety.

Gap in the market

Restaurants don’t solve it

Most Indian restaurants offer takeaway but not tailored festival-style menus for 20–100 guests, delivered hot and ready to serve. Authentic Nepali food options are even more limited.

Our solution

Aroma Catering

We cook authentic Nepali & Indian food in a licensed commercial kitchen and deliver it hot, in bulk, straight to the event. Hosts get traditional taste with professional handling – and can finally enjoy their own celebration.

Business Model

Lean operations, clear path to profit

Aroma Catering is built on the Business Model Canvas: clear value proposition, targeted customer segments, and a lean cost base.

Target group & channels

  • Primary: Nepali & Indian communities in Uusimaa (Helsinki, Espoo, Vantaa).
  • Secondary: student unions, NGOs, multicultural companies.
  • Channels: WhatsApp groups, Instagram, Facebook, flyers at temples and ethnic stores, word-of-mouth.

Customer relationship

  • Direct orders via WhatsApp, Instagram DMs and phone.
  • Customisable menus per event.
  • Loyalty pricing for repeat families and associations.

Revenue & costs

  • Revenue: plate-based pricing + add-ons + delivery.
  • Fixed costs: ~1 300 €/month (kitchen, utilities, basic marketing).
  • Variable costs: ~4.60 €/plate (ingredients + packaging).
  • Break-even: ≈ 300 plates/month.

Key resources & partners

  • Licensed commercial kitchen and equipment.
  • Four founders with Hygiene Passports.
  • Partners: kitchen landlord, 24Rent, Kespro + ethnic stores, cutlery rental companies, community associations.
